**Strategic/Technical Commentary:** The "3-to-1" penalty isn't just about surface area; it's about the **TSV (Through-Silicon Via)** penalty. HBM isn't a flat chip; it's a skyscraper of 8 or 12 DRAM dies stacked vertically. To connect them, manufacturers have to drill thousands of microscopic holes (vias) through the silicon itself.

1. **Lost Area:** These holes eat up physical space on the die where memory cells *should* be, reducing the density of each layer.  
2. **Yield Killers:** Drilling holes through fragile silicon is a great way to crack the wafer. If one layer in an 8-high stack fails, the whole stack is often trash. This yield loss exacerbates the shortage. They have to print a lot more wafers to get the same amount of working gigabytes compared to standard flat RAM.

The “Grid” bottleneck isn't just the assembly of the transformer but the **Grain-Oriented Electrical Steel (GOES)** inside it.
